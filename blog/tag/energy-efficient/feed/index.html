]]> With lighting controls, you can control the lighting in your house with the touch of a button. Gone are the days of pesky light switches and only getting one level of brightness. Let’s take a look at some of the benefits of using lighting controls in West Palm Beach.

Saves Energy

Every dimmer automatically saves 4-9% in electricity, even at the highest lighting levels, over a standard on-off switch. When users choose to dim their lights even more electricity is saved. Basically, the more you dim, the more you save. Traditional light switches don’t give you the ability to dim the lights.

Saves Money

Saving energy also results in saving on energy bill costs. Lighting control systems can be set turn on lights according to changes in light or the time of day. This can save money because your lights won’t be running at full strength around the clock.

Extends the Life of the Bulb

Another way lighting controls can save money is with the actual light bulb itself. With the ability to dim the lights at the touch of a button, you can extend the life of the light bulb, thus cutting down on how often you have to replace them.

]]> Three Things to Consider When Switching to LED Bulbs

LED bulbs have been available to the mass market for a few years now and can provide your home with an ultra-energy efficient light source. Additionally, once you buy an LED light bulb, you may never have to replace it. LED bulbs have an estimated life span of 20 to 25 years (the incandescent light bulb has an average life span of one year.) These bulbs are estimated to save you about 85% of the average energy cost, during the lifetime of the bulb. That adds up when you think of all the light sources in your home!

LED light bulbs keep getting better, and less expensive. Prices are constantly changing and can vary from $18 to $50 a bulb. But not all LED lights are created equal. Before you invest in this wonder bulb, make sure you purchase the type that is right for your home.

Three things to consider when purchasing LED lights:

1. Are they dimmable? Dimmable lighting is usually preferred (it can set the atmosphere, and helps cut down on energy output.) Make sure you purchase LED lights that are specifically made for diming. If they do not specify this feature, you will have issues with shut off and flickering.

2. Color temperature. LED lights are sometimes described as warm white or cool white, with a number to indicate how warm or cool. Depending on what part of your home you are lighting you may want a warmer light, more yellowish like daylight, or a cooler brighter light. Color temperature measurements vary high to low, higher temperatures 5500 K or more are “cool” blue to green colors and lower temperatures (2700 – 3500 K) are warm yellow to red colors. The warmer colors more closely match the incandescent bulbs.

3. Lumen output. This is the amount of lighting that the bulb puts out. If you are replacing a 60 watt-light bulb, you want to look for a lumen output in the 800 range; a 40-watt light bulb lumen output is 400 to 450.
