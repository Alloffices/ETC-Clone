]]> As football season starts up, you may be in the market for a new TV – one on the cutting edge of technology.  The latest and greatest is 4K Ultra HD TV’s.

What is Ultra HD or 4K? 

4K describes the amount of pixels shown on the screen.  The current HD is 1080p showing 2 million pixels, 4K shows 8.3 million pixels.   These numbers boil down to more detail.  The clarity of Ultra HD on a 70 inch TV is amazing – four times the clarity of HD!  In fact, Wimbledon was just filmed in 4K with some small advertisements on one of the tennis player’s fingernails to show off the amazing clarity.

Why should you care about 4K?

Although 4K content is not readily available right now, it is coming.  Many movies are being shot in 4K and even 5K formats.  Some blockbuster movies like Spiderman have been converted into 4K versions.  And sporting events are starting to be filmed in Ultra HD (Super Bowl 2013 was shot in 4K), allowing officials to see even the closest call on a football field or tennis court.  Because the resolution is so high, a small section of the picture can be focused on with unbelievable clarity, making it easy to make a call, in or out.  The World Cup 2014 will be filmed in Ultra HD, providing a TV experience like no other.

Regardless of media content, you will notice the difference immediately as the HD content is converted to the new 4K sharper picture.  Yes, even 4 times sharper than today’s HD.  And the larger the TV screen, the more noticeable the difference.  It is the future of TV.

It is expected that Ultra HD content will become more prevalent over the next three to five years.  In fact many of the most popular shows on TV are currently filmed in 4K, and your Ultra HD TV will “up-convert” those shows so you can watch the show with four times the clarity than your current HD experience.  Businesses like Netflix expect to have Ultra HD streaming via the internet within two years, although other streaming sources may provide access to converted 4K movies and shows sooner.

Another 4K advantage is the ability to see 3D content glasses free. The number of pixels makes the ability of glasses free 3D TV more of a reality than with standard HD content.

All that said the biggest hurdle to the new TV’s is price.  But as of August 2013, the prices and screen sizes are coming down.  We will see new Ultra HD TV’s from different manufacturers this fall, with prices in the $3,500 to $7,000 range, maybe even less.  Read a review on Sony’s 4K TV here.

Although 4K is an emerging technology, it is will be the new standard of TV, changing the way we watch movies and sporting events, again.  Will you be an early adopter?

New fiber-optic based technology can detect an intruder on the perimeter of your property before they ever get close to your home or business. This update to old fashioned security measures such as fencing, barrier walls and security cameras is now readily available.

One product we are excited about is FD322 Fiber-Optic Perimeter Detection System, created by Fiber SenSys. This new value priced edition offers a rich feature set at a price that opens up the availability to more home owners and commercial businesses.

This new technology detects intruders who are attempting to climb over, crawl under or cut through perimeter fence or barrier. An alarm processing unit detects every movement of the sensing fiber extended along the perimeter fence and uses advanced processing algorithms to carefully distinguish between intruders and “nuisance alarms” that might be caused by wind, falling palm fronds, or small animals. And unlike the current wiring systems that use metallic sensors, the system’s fiber-optic based sensing elements are unaffected by harsh environments including UV radiation, moisture, salt, snow or even lightning strikes.

Since these systems use fiber-cabling, it is important that installers have installation experience and knowledge of fiber-optics. Proper tuning and calibration of the systems is important to ensure accurate and reliable intrusion detection.
