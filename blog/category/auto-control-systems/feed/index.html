]]> Saving energy is important in any size household.  New automated technology is coming on the market to make it easier and easier to do.  Not only can home automation systems reduce the amount of energy used in your household, it can be controlled remotely from anywhere in the world as well.

One simple automation appliance that is getting attention right now is the new “smart” thermostats. 

Most households have a thermostat that you can program in advance to warm or cool the house according to your family’s weekly schedule.  The next generation of thermostat is “smart” –  it can sense if people are in the room, begin to predict patterns of activity and adjust temperatures accordingly.  This thermostat can also be controlled from a smart phone, computer or tablet.

These types of smart gadgets certainly reduce wasted energy in the home, but the data is still being gathered to prove how significant the savings can be.  Regardless of energy savings, ease of use is certainly a plus.  Wasting energy cooling an empty house because you forgot to adjust the pre-set schedule before you left for vacation is definitely a thing of the past. Access your HVAC control through your smartphone or tablet and you can adjust in seconds and not worry one second more.

These “smart” thermostats can also send an alert when temperatures exceed a certain level so that if the A/C unit were to fail, you can be notified to save humidity damage.  They can also monitor each unit  so that if someone were to turn down the A/C too low, thinking it will cool faster, the thermostat can be automatically reset to the desired high/low setting.

And this simple control is starting to become more than just a way to control your home temperature.  Google bought the popular “smart” thermostat company Nest.  Expect to see even more changes in the things your thermostat can do in the next few years.

Although the materials used to construct your home can make a difference to your energy consumption, the technology and what you do on the inside is what matters in the long term.

One example of an easy-to-use integrated control system: Savant Remote and iPad

Automated And Integrated Control Systems

The key to success in your home is using technology to maximize efficiency – making things more energy efficient and managing the energy you use.  Having one control system that integrates all the different technology in your home makes it easy to be eco-friendly.

The control systems available today are so intuitive that children and guests can use them without looking at a manual or having to endure a lengthy explanation.  And you can do so much more than just turn things on and off.  Using a system that interfaces with your iPhone, iPad or tablet, you can dim lights, manage multiple room systems like TV’s or audio, climate control, motorized shades, landscape lighting, the pool system, and security – from home or anywhere in the world.

For example, as you leave your home to go out for the evening, you can press one button that says “Away” and the security system is activated and all the lights in the home turn off.  This not only saves the time of going room to room to shut lights off, it also saves energy, because no lights are left on by mistake.

In addition to making energy management easy, home technology integration also makes monitoring energy use simple.  You can see real time energy consumption for your entire home day by day, down to each outlet in the home. ]]> A few of  ETC’s top system designers attended CEDIA 2011.  Here are three items that caught their attention:

Crestron

The home automation product that made the biggest impact in relation to the ETC client was the new Crestron Fusion EM.  It is a module that provides real time feedback on energy usage and much more including:

  • Track real-time energy usage (and how it relates to dollars)
  • View historical energy consumption for day, week, month or year
  • Control lighting levels, shades, and climate in real time (it’s amazing how much energy a window treatment can save)
  • Change settings for occupied and unoccupied states (i.e. in residence, out of residence)
  • Adjust demand response rules for lighting and HVAC

This information can be viewed and adjusted on touch panels, computers or mobile device.

Lutron

Lutron introduced a new automated entry-level window treatment, Sivoia® QS Wireless cellular shades. The new automated shades (accordion style) provide an affordable solution that can be wired into a standard control system or can run wirelessly using a battery.  The wireless control system is powered by 6 D batteries when combined with Lutron’s Triatholon power technology will provide a three year battery life.

Three types of shades are available including the blackout shade for theater rooms, single and double cell shades that allow some light to pass through.  Sizes range from 3’ x 5’ to 82” x 82” with more than 100 color and pattern combinations.

Control4

Control4 showed a handful of new home technology products that are being introduced early 2012. One highlight is a replacement for their wireless 7” touch panel.  This new model has a built-in camera and microphone.  This will allow video intercom with other touch panels.  Eventually, Control4’s in-wall versions (which can audio intercom amongst one another) will have cameras as well.
